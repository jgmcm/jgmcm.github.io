    <p class="lede">I am a Dame Kathleen Ollerenshaw Fellow at the <a href="https://www.graphene.manchester.ac.uk/">National Graphene Institute</a>, University of Manchester.</p>

    <p>My research centres on two-dimensional materials and their heterostructures. Current topics include moiré physics and correlated phenomena in twisted TMDs, stacking-engineered ferroelectricity and multiferroics, high-throughput DFT screening of emerging 2D compounds, and machine-learning interatomic potentials.</p>

## Research Themes

<div class="theme-grid">
  <section>
    <h3>Layered Materials</h3>
    <p>First-principles modelling of electronic, structural, and electromechanical behaviour in 2D compounds and heterostructures.</p>
  </section>
  <section>
    <h3>Twistronics</h3>
    <p>Moiré reconstruction, boundary networks, and correlated electronic phases in misaligned van der Waals systems.</p>
  </section>
  <section>
    <h3>Stacking Order</h3>
    <p>Ferroelectric, magnetic, and multiferroic responses engineered through stacking sequence, registry, and strain.</p>
  </section>
  <section>
    <h3>Atomistic ML</h3>
    <p>Graph-neural-network potentials, charge-density prediction, and optimisation workflows for large-scale materials discovery.</p>
  </section>
